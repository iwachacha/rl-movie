# Common Failures

Use this file to diagnose why an artifact feels weaker than its implementation effort suggests.

## Concept Failures

- The premise sounds like a benchmark, not a video.
- The rule needs too much explanation before the viewer can care.
- Success is technically meaningful but visually boring.
- There is no obvious danger, pressure, or absurd contrast.

## Scene Failures

- The agent blends into the environment.
- The goal object is not visually privileged.
- Lighting and dressing add mood but reduce readability.
- The scene looks polished in stills but muddy in motion.

## Footage Failures

- All clips show the same kind of near-success.
- The camera is close but unreadable, or wide but emotionally flat.
- The best behavior has no clean take.
- The reset choreography is dead time.

## Package Failures

- The title describes the algorithm instead of the action.
- The thumbnail needs too much text to make sense.
- The cold open shows action but not the question.
- The edit contains several "kind of improving" clips with no leap.

## Script Failures

- The narration repeats what the viewer can already see.
- The tone lane does not match the footage.
- The lines are too long for TTS rhythm.
- The script carries the whole video because the footage is not self-explanatory.

## Review Rule

When several failures appear, find the upstream one first.
If the concept is weak, do not spend most of the review on thumbnail polish.
