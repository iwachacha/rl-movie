# Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to judge whether an artifact is strong enough to keep investing in as video.
Score only the categories that the current stage can actually prove.

## Verdict Bands

- `Ready`: strong enough to move forward without a major reframe
- `Promising but blocked`: good core, but one or two issues are still suppressing the video's strength
- `Not video-ready yet`: the current artifact does not yet show a compelling viewer promise

## Core Categories

## 1. Hook Clarity

Ask:
- can a new viewer identify the actor quickly?
- can they tell what success looks like?
- can they see what can go wrong?

Strong:
- actor, goal, and danger are readable in under five seconds
- the concept can be pitched in one sentence

Weak:
- the viewer must hear an explanation before the action makes sense
- the scene has no obvious conflict or target

## 2. Failure Entertainment

Ask:
- are failures funny, tense, awkward, or dramatic?
- do failures differ enough to feel like escalation instead of repetition?

Strong:
- at least one failure pattern is memorable on its own
- resets are still watchable

Weak:
- failure just looks like nothing happening
- every miss feels visually identical

## 3. Progress Or Payoff

Ask:
- is there a visible breakthrough, exploit, or clean solve?
- can a viewer feel the policy change without reading metrics?

Strong:
- there is a distinct "it got it" moment
- the payoff answers the cold open or premise

Weak:
- progress is only visible in charts or reward summaries
- the final behavior is technically correct but visually flat

## 4. Visual And Camera Read

Ask:
- can the eye find the agent, goal, and hazard immediately?
- does the camera show cause and effect?

Strong:
- silhouettes, contrast, landmarks, and framing all support readability
- at least one recording angle could plausibly become the thumbnail

Weak:
- the background competes with the action
- framing hides why the agent succeeded or failed

## 5. Package Strength

Ask:
- does the title promise the same thing the thumbnail shows?
- does the cold open raise the right question fast?
- does the beat order preserve momentum?

Strong:
- title, thumbnail, and first beat all support one viewer promise
- there is one clear hero arc

Weak:
- multiple arcs compete for the same video
- the title depends on RL jargon to be interesting

## 6. Script Fit

Ask:
- does narration clarify, compress, or sharpen?
- does the spoken copy sound natural in TTS?

Strong:
- lines are short, concrete, and timed to what the viewer sees
- silence is used when the footage already carries the moment

Weak:
- narration explains the joke before it lands
- long sentences fight the footage or sound robotic

## 7. Effort-To-Value Ratio

Ask:
- is this artifact strong enough to justify the next chunk of work?
- would a smaller change create a bigger lift than a large rebuild?

Strong:
- the next step is obvious and scoped
- current momentum is worth protecting

Weak:
- major additional work is needed just to make the concept legible
- the remaining lift is expensive but low impact on watchability
