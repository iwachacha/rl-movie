# Visual Lanes

Use this file to choose a coherent visual direction before touching props, lights, or volumes.
Pick one lane first. Only blend lanes when there is a clear reason.

## 1. Broadcast Arena

Use when:
- the scenario is an obstacle course, hazard room, arena, lane challenge, or timing puzzle
- the viewer must read the whole loop in seconds
- training progress matters more than atmosphere

Look for:
- strong separation between agent, goal, hazard, and floor
- simple floor materials with clear edge contrast
- restrained background dressing
- one or two accent colors only
- landmarks that help orientation without cluttering the play lane

Borrow:
- the clarity and bold contrast of successful `AI learns` videos
- arenas that remain readable when sped up or viewed on mobile

Avoid:
- busy textures under the agent
- decorative props inside the main movement lane
- dark corners that hide failure causes

## 2. Stylized Setpiece

Use when:
- the environment fantasy is part of the hook
- the video needs more spectacle than a plain arena
- hazards, props, or reset moments should feel dramatic

Look for:
- stronger depth layering
- one dominant mood and palette
- clustered dressing near edges, corners, or backdrop planes
- controlled use of fog, emissives, particles, or decals
- one memorable background silhouette

Borrow:
- set-dressed scenes where the environment upgrades the gimmick
- atmosphere that supports the story without hiding the action

Avoid:
- evenly sprinkling props everywhere
- multiple competing color stories
- particles or fog crossing the core decision space

## 3. Simulation Documentary

Use when:
- the system itself is the spectacle
- viewers should observe behavior patterns, not just one high-drama moment
- the scene needs to feel polished and intentional rather than loud

Look for:
- calmer palette and simpler materials
- readable boundaries and landmarks
- elegant negative space
- softer atmosphere with high behavioral legibility
- visual cues that explain the system structure

Borrow:
- polished simulation channels where causality is easy to follow
- composition that makes the experiment feel credible and beautiful

Avoid:
- sterile empty-box scenes with no scale cues
- dramatic grading that makes states harder to compare
- clutter that suggests realism but adds no understanding

## Quick Choice Rule

- If in doubt, choose `Broadcast Arena`.
- Move to `Stylized Setpiece` only when the hook clearly benefits from atmosphere.
- Choose `Simulation Documentary` when the appeal is emergent behavior over danger spectacle.
