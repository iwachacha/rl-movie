# Idea Lenses

Use these lenses to widen the search space without drifting into unbuildable concepts.
Mix one emotional lane with one spectacle lever, then sanity-check with the feasibility ladder.

## Emotional Lanes

- Funny incompetence: clumsy jobs, overcorrecting robots, awkward household disasters, tiny failures that snowball.
- Impressive mastery: impossible-looking deliveries, precision navigation, elegant recoveries, perfect timing under pressure.
- Weird and uncanny: behavior that feels almost human but slightly wrong, ritualized motion, eerie repetition, toy-scale strangeness.
- Tense and scary: pursuit, countdowns, unstable platforms, fragile cargo, dim spaces, one-hit failures, escalating hazards.
- Satisfying and hypnotic: sorting, sweeping, stacking, lining up, domino-like chain reactions, near-perfect cleanup.
- Tragic chaos: everything is almost under control until one mistake causes a hilarious or horrifying collapse.

## Spectacle Levers

- Scale mismatch: tiny agent in giant machinery, huge object carried by a tiny body, oversized hazards.
- Fragility: glass, eggs, dishes, unstable towers, precious glowing core, wobbling payloads.
- Chain reaction: one hit triggers alarms, collapsing routes, moving crowd props, spreading fire-light substitutes, tumbling clutter.
- Near miss: doors closing, trains passing, crushers, lasers, swinging obstacles, falling props.
- Social misunderstanding: scripted NPCs interpret the agent's actions in a funny or ominous way.
- False safety: calm environment with hidden traps that activate late.
- Ritual or ceremony: the agent performs a task that looks strangely meaningful even though the mechanics are simple.

## Feasibility Ladder

- Easy: one agent, one room or lane, one core objective, limited physics, minimal asset needs.
- Moderate: one agent plus several hazard types, staged props, a few randomization points, richer fail states.
- Stretch but feasible: one strong central gimmick plus custom scripted events or more demanding camera choreography.
- Reject or reduce: ideas that need novel research, many fully autonomous actors, or high-fidelity simulation to be interesting.

## Simplification Patterns

- Replace crowds with a few scripted mannequins or moving blockers.
- Replace fluid or fire simulation with moving hazard volumes, particles, and timed penalties.
- Replace natural language goals with icons, colored beacons, or simple state machines.
- Replace dexterous manipulation with magnetic pickup zones, snap points, or push/carry abstractions.
- Replace open-world complexity with one dense arena that contains multiple dramatic beats.
- Replace "smart enemies" with predictable patrols plus one surprising event trigger.

## Fast Smell Test

Ask these questions before recommending an idea:

1. Can a viewer understand the joke, tension, or challenge in under 10 seconds?
2. Is the success state visually obvious?
3. Is failure visually entertaining?
4. Can the core mechanic be implemented with ordinary Unity objects, triggers, animation, and ML-Agents control?
5. Can the premise survive after removing the most technically risky part?

If the answer to `4` or `5` is no, simplify the concept before offering it.
