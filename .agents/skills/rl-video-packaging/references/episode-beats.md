# Episode Beats

Use this file to turn raw RL footage into a short, clear story.
The default assumption for this project is a short-form or tightly edited video, not a long tutorial.

## Default Beat Stack

1. Cold open
2. Rule or premise
3. Early chaos
4. Breakthrough
5. Payoff
6. Optional comparison or ending button

## 1. Cold Open

Show the weirdest, funniest, or tensest 1-2 moments first.
The viewer should immediately understand:
- who is acting
- what they are trying to do
- what can go wrong

Good cold open options:
- near-success followed by failure
- ridiculous failure with obvious stakes
- the final solve teased before explanation
- a strange exploit that begs for context

Bad cold open options:
- a calm establishing shot with no action
- UI-heavy training info
- narration that explains before the viewer has seen anything

## 2. Rule Or Premise

Explain only enough to decode the scene.
Usually one sentence or one text card is enough.

Good:
- `Push the core into the socket before the beam hits.`
- `The AI has to cross without falling into the void.`

Bad:
- reward shaping explanations
- observation/action space summaries
- long lore setup

## 3. Early Chaos

Show failure as entertainment, not as dead time.
Pick 1-3 clips that fail in clearly different ways:
- dumb collision
- hesitation
- greedy shortcut
- panic near the goal

## 4. Breakthrough

This is the first clip where the viewer feels the policy "got it" or discovered something worth retelling.
Prefer a visible leap over subtle incremental progress.

## 5. Payoff

Use the cleanest, most legible success or best near-success.
The payoff should feel like the answer to the cold open.

## 6. Comparison Or Button

Use only if it adds clarity.

Good endings:
- bad early vs clean final on matching angles
- one last funny failure after success
- a quiet flex shot showing competence

Bad endings:
- several redundant victory clips
- a metric screen as the final image
