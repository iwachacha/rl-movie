# Footage Selection

Use this file when choosing which clips deserve the final edit.

## Clip Roles

Keep a candidate pool for:
- `Hero clip`: the single most watchable moment
- `Fail clip`: the funniest or clearest bad outcome
- `Breakthrough clip`: the first obviously smarter behavior
- `Payoff clip`: the clean final success or near-success
- `Comparison clip`: same angle, different result

## Selection Rules

Choose clips that are:
- readable without debug overlays
- behaviorally distinct
- short enough to keep pace
- emotionally legible

Cut clips that are:
- technically better but visually ambiguous
- redundant with a stronger clip
- only interesting after explanation

## Training Progress Rule

Do not try to show every stage.
Usually 3 meaningful jumps are enough:
- bad
- better
- best

## Comparison Rule

Use matched framing whenever possible.
If the before and after angles are too different, the comparison loses force.

## Missing-Asset Callout

If the package is weak because footage is missing, say exactly what needs to be captured:
- one cleaner wide success
- one follow clip with body language
- one failure from the main readable angle
- one thumbnail-ready frame
